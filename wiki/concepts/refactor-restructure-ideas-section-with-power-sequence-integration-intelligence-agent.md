# Refactor: Restructure Ideas Section with Power Sequence + Integration Intelligence Agent

This refactor merges previous ideas into a unified "Integration Intelligence Agent" concept, prioritizing it in a new 'power sequence' for optimal conversation flow. The agent's focus has been specifically reframed from GTM tracking to data schema normalization to align with infrastructure-level integration needs. These changes aim to present project ideas more strategically, particularly for discussions with key advisors.

## Key Concepts

Integration Intelligence Agent,Power Sequence,Data Schema Normalization,AI Discoverability,DataChat/NL Actions,GTM Tracking

## Details

This commit, part of the [[Career-Datacenter]] repository, introduces a significant restructuring of project ideas to optimize their presentation and strategic alignment. The primary change involves consolidating former ideas 1 and 2 into a single, more robust concept: the **Integration Intelligence Agent**. This agent is envisioned with a three-phase lifecycle: Recon, Schema Normalization, and Drift Monitoring.

A key aspect of this refactor is the reordering of ideas into a "power sequence" for more effective conversation flow. The [[Integration Intelligence Agent]] is now positioned first, emphasizing its grounded and operational nature. This is followed by [[AI Discoverability]] (focusing on the ecosystem), then [[DataChat/NL Actions]] (product-oriented), with operational agents trailing as supporting threads.

Critically, the **Integration Intelligence Agent**'s core purpose has been reframed. Its previous emphasis on GTM tracking has been shifted to **data schema normalization**. This strategic pivot acknowledges that GTM is within the domain of [[Conor Winders]], while the new focus aligns with [[Rogers]]' expertise in integration data infrastructure, particularly at the scale of 1,000+ marketplace adapters.

The changes also include adding an advisor's exact opening line for introducing the lead idea and an explicit warning not to use the GTM framing when discussing with Rogers. The conversation flow in Section 6 and the Quick Reference Card have been updated to reflect this new idea sequence and include per-idea caveats.
