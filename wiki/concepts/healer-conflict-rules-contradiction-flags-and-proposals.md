# Healer: Conflict Rules, Contradiction Flags, and Proposals

This commit introduces a sophisticated 'Healer' mechanism within the Brain Project to manage conflicts between AI-generated content and human edits. It establishes a 3-tier human sovereignty system: `direct_edit` for untouched AI files, `append_synthesis` for files with stale human edits, and `skip` for recently edited files to prevent premature overwrites. The system explicitly flags contradictions rather than auto-resolving them, ensuring human review for critical discrepancies.

## Key Concepts

Human Sovereignty,AI-Human Collaboration,Conflict Resolution,Contradiction Flags,Healer Mechanism,Automated Knowledge System Maintenance

## Details

This feature, implemented in the `[[Brain Project]]` (commit `779b295`, authored by [[Rahil Singhi]] on 2026-04-04), enhances the system's ability to manage dynamic content generated by AI, specifically in collaboration with human input. It introduces a `Healer` component that applies a 3-tier human sovereignty policy:

*   **`direct_edit`**: For files that have been solely modified by the AI and have no human edits, allowing the AI to update them directly.
*   **`append_synthesis`**: For files where human edits exist but are considered 'stale' (e.g., older than a certain threshold, implying the human edit might no longer be current or comprehensive). In this case, the AI's proposal is appended or synthesized with the existing content, preserving human contributions while integrating new AI insights.
*   **`skip`**: For files that have recent human edits (e.g., within a 24-hour cooldown period). This prevents the AI from immediately overwriting or modifying content that a human is actively working on or has recently finalized, giving precedence to recent human decisions.

Crucially, the system is designed to handle contradictions by always producing a `flag_contradiction`. This means that if the AI identifies conflicting information between its generated content and existing human edits, it will *never* auto-resolve the conflict. Instead, it explicitly marks the contradiction, necessitating human intervention and review to resolve the discrepancy. This design choice prioritizes human oversight in critical knowledge areas, reflecting a strong emphasis on [[Human Sovereignty]] within the AI-human collaborative workflow.

This enhancement contributes to the robustness and maintainability of the knowledge base, especially as noted in discussions around `[[Brain Project Phase 2 Hardening: Embed Safety, Connection Discovery, and Healer Tests (33a3510)]]` and `[[Brain Repository: Pre-Heal Snapshot (2026-04-09)]]`, ensuring that the [[Automated Knowledge System Maintenance]] processes are both efficient and respectful of human contributions.
