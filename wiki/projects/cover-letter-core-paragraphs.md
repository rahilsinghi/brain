# Cover Letter Core Paragraphs

A modular library of reusable cover letter building blocks designed for tailored job applications. Templates are organized by section — opening hooks, company alignment, proof points, and closings — and can be mixed and matched based on the target role and company. Includes tailoring instructions and references to external evidence sources like experience records and bullet banks.

## Key Concepts

- **Modular Templates**: Interchangeable paragraph blocks for each section of a cover letter
- **Opening Hooks**: Two styles — Direct/Enthusiastic and Narrative/Problem-Focused
- **Why This Company**: Templates for Mission Alignment and Technical Admiration
- **Proof Points**: Three types — Technical Depth, Impact Focus, and Collaboration/Ownership
- **Evidence IDs**: References to external data sources (e.g., `exp_kismet`, `proj_example_001`) for pulling quantified achievements
- **Closing Templates**: Two versions — Forward-Looking and Mutual Benefit
- **Tailoring Workflow**: Step-by-step process for customizing templates to a specific job description

## Details

## Overview

This resource provides a structured set of reusable paragraph templates for constructing tailored cover letters quickly and consistently. Templates are parameterized with placeholders (e.g., `[ROLE]`, `[COMPANY]`, `[METRIC]`) and should be customized using job description research and personal evidence.

---

## Opening Hook Templates

### Version 1: Direct + Enthusiastic
Best for roles at companies with a clear, well-known mission. Lead with identity, relevant domain experience, and enthusiasm for the company's specific goal.

> *I'm writing to apply for the [ROLE] position at [COMPANY]. As an MS Computer Engineering student at NYU Tandon with experience in [RELEVANT DOMAIN], I'm excited by [COMPANY]'s mission to [SPECIFIC COMPANY GOAL] and believe my background in [KEY STRENGTH] makes me a strong fit.*

### Version 2: Narrative / Problem-Focused
Best for roles where storytelling and passion for a domain are differentiators. Opens with a personal anecdote that connects to the company's work.

> *When I [BRIEF ANECDOTE SHOWING PASSION FOR DOMAIN], I realized [INSIGHT]. That's why I'm drawn to [COMPANY]'s work on [SPECIFIC PRODUCT/MISSION], and why I'm applying for the [ROLE] position.*

---

## Why This Company

### Mission Alignment
Use when you have specific knowledge of the company's mission, products, or public content (blog posts, launches, open-source projects).

> *[COMPANY]'s focus on [SPECIFIC MISSION/PRODUCT] resonates with me because [PERSONAL CONNECTION]. I've followed [SPECIFIC THING: product launch, blog post, open-source project] and am impressed by [SPECIFIC DETAIL].*

### Technical Admiration
Use when applying to engineering-heavy roles where you can demonstrate awareness of the company's technical challenges.

> *I'm particularly excited by [COMPANY]'s technical challenges around [SPECIFIC AREA: scale, ML infrastructure, distributed systems]. Having worked on [MY RELEVANT EXPERIENCE], I understand the complexity of [SHARED CHALLENGE] and would love to contribute to solving it.*

## Proof Points

Select 2–3 proof points per letter that best match the job description requirements. Reference `data/experience.yaml` and `docs/bullet_bank.md` for quantified evidence.

### Proof Point 1: Technical Depth
Highlights a specific technical achievement with a measurable outcome.

> *At [COMPANY/PROJECT], I [SPECIFIC TECHNICAL ACHIEVEMENT WITH METRIC]. This experience taught me [KEY SKILL] and prepared me to [RELEVANT TASK FOR NEW ROLE].*

**Evidence IDs:**
- `exp_kismet` → "Built API serving 10K+ requests/day"
- `proj_example_001` → "Developed ML model with 92% F1 score"

### Proof Point 2: Impact Focus
Demonstrates results-oriented mindset with quantified outcomes.

> *I'm driven by measurable impact. In my role at [COMPANY], I [ACTION] that resulted in [QUANTIFIED RESULT: 40% latency reduction, 10K users, 99.9% uptime]. I bring this same results-oriented mindset to every project.*

### Proof Point 3: Collaboration / Ownership
Highlights cross-functional work and leadership capability.

> *I thrive in collaborative environments where ownership is valued. At [COMPANY], I [EXAMPLE OF CROSS-FUNCTIONAL WORK OR LEADERSHIP], demonstrating my ability to [RELEVANT SOFT SKILL: communicate with stakeholders, drive projects end-to-end].*

## Closing Templates

### Version 1: Forward-Looking
Strong, confident close that ties key skills to a specific team goal.

> *I'd love the opportunity to bring my skills in [KEY SKILL 1] and [KEY SKILL 2] to [COMPANY] and contribute to [SPECIFIC TEAM GOAL]. Thank you for considering my application — I look forward to discussing how I can add value to your team.*

### Version 2: Mutual Benefit
More humble and collaborative tone; good for senior teams or learning-focused roles.

> *I'm excited about the opportunity to learn from [COMPANY]'s [TEAM/LEADERS] while contributing my expertise in [DOMAIN]. I'd welcome the chance to discuss how my background aligns with your needs.*

## Tailoring Instructions

1. **Read the JD carefully** and identify:
   - Company mission and product specifics
   - Key technical challenges mentioned
   - Must-have and nice-to-have skills
2. **Choose one opening hook** template based on tone fit
3. **Customize the "Why This Company" paragraph** using recent research: company blog, LinkedIn, product pages, or news
4. **Select 2–3 proof points** that directly mirror JD requirements; pull evidence from `data/experience.yaml` and `docs/bullet_bank.md`
5. **Choose a closing template** based on desired tone
6. **Keep total length to 3–4 short paragraphs**, under 300 words
7. **Proofread** for accuracy of company name, role title, and all placeholder substitutions
