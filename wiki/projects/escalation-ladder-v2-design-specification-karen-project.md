# Escalation Ladder V2 Design Specification (Karen Project)

This document outlines the design specification for version 2 of the escalation ladder within the Karen Project, introducing a new 10-level structure with unique channels per level. It details the removal of outdated communication methods like LinkedIn and Twitter, and the addition of new channels such as OSINT research, Slack, real Calendar integration, and FedEx rate quotes.

## Key Concepts

Escalation Ladder,Channel-based Communication,OSINT Research,Slack Integration,Calendar Integration,FedEx Rate Quotes,Documentation Updates,Environmental Variables,De-escalation Sequence

## Details

The **Escalation Ladder V2 Design Specification** for the [[Karen Project]] introduces a significantly revised 10-level escalation framework. A core principle of this new design is to ensure each level utilizes a unique communication channel, preventing repeats and streamlining the escalation process.

Key changes include:

*   **Removed Channels**: LinkedIn (previously a stub) and Twitter (due to broken 402 errors) have been removed from the escalation sequence.
*   **Added Channels**: 
    *   **Level 4 (L4)**: Integration of **OSINT research** capabilities.
    *   **Level 6 (L6)**: Introduction of **Slack** for communication.
    *   **Level 9 (L9)**: Implementation of a real **Calendar** for scheduling and coordination.
    *   **Level 10 (L10)**: Inclusion of **FedEx rate quotes** for logistics-related escalations.

Accompanying these structural changes, the related project documentation has been thoroughly updated. This includes the `CLAUDE.md` file, specifically its ladder section, environmental variables, and the accounts table. Additionally, the de-escalation sequence has been reviewed and updated to align with the new 10-level framework.
