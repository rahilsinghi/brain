# Wiki Scanner for Graph Data Extraction (Brain Project)

This feature introduces a wiki scanner capable of extracting nodes, links, and connection counts from wiki content within the Brain Project. It enhances the system's ability to automatically parse and structure knowledge graph data from raw text.

## Key Concepts

Wiki Scanner,Knowledge Graph Extraction,Node Extraction,Link Extraction,Connection Counting,Automated Data Ingestion

## Details

The `f6de304` commit to the `rahilsinghi/brain` repository implements a new wiki scanner feature. This scanner is designed to automatically process wiki content, identifying and extracting key components for building and maintaining a knowledge graph. Specifically, it extracts individual 'nodes' (concepts or entities), 'links' (relationships between nodes), and quantifies 'connection counts' (the number of links associated with each node).

This enhancement is crucial for the [[Brain Project]]'s ability to ingest raw information and convert it into a structured, navigable graph. The commit involved changes to 2 files, adding 306 lines of code, and was authored by Rahil Singhi, with co-authorship from Claude Opus 4.6.
