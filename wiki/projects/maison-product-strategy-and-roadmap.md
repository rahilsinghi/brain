# Maison: Product Strategy and Roadmap

This document outlines Maison's product strategy, centered around a "commercial operating system" for hotels, powered by a three-agent framework: Marketing, Sales, and Support. Key strategic pillars include an API-first approach, robust integrations, and a focus on lead capture and AI-native conversion, informed by continuous client feedback and structured weekly product planning.

## Details

Maison positions itself as a **commercial operating system** specifically designed for hotels, built upon a foundational **three-agent framework**:

### Product Vision — Three Agents Framework
1.  **[[Marketing Agent]] (AI visibility + content flywheel)**:
    *   Transforms approved hotel content into a definitive source of truth.
    *   Facilitates [[AI-native conversion]], allowing guests to discover, evaluate, and complete direct bookings within their personal AI.
    *   Identifies missing or unclear information and suggests new content (e.g., [[FAQs]], policies, structured data) to publish.
2.  **[[Sales Agent]] (conversion + lead capture)**:
    *   Streamlines the booking process to reduce friction.
    *   Captures high-intent inquiries for groups, weddings, meetings, buyouts, and longer stays.
    *   Converts consideration into qualified leads or direct bookings.
3.  **[[Support Agent]] (deflection + workload reduction)**:
    *   Handles common pre-stay questions (e.g., parking, check-in/out, pet policy, breakfast).
    *   Provides instant answers in the guest's preferred language, reducing staff interruptions.

### [[API-First Future]]
*   [[Fredrik]] emphasizes an "[[API-first]]" approach as fundamental to [[Maison Labs]]' future, drawing parallels to [[Hopper]]'s partnership with [[Capital One]].
*   Focuses on [[MCP]] (Mobile/Maison Communication Protocol) and [[A2A protocols]] for seamless [[AI]] agent interoperability.

### Key Product Discussions
*   **Agents terminology**: Differentiating between internal (Tool → Skill → Agent) and external (Pre-built Agent → Custom Agent) terminologies.
*   **[[Schema markup / JSON-LD]]**: Identified as critical for [[AI]] visibility, requiring team-wide training.
*   **[[WebMCP]]**: Exploring the browser's capability to function as an [[MCP]].
*   **[[Lead capture]]**: A growing request from hotels for contact capture functionality.
*   **[[Source ingestion]]**: Plans to automatically ingest profiles from booking.com and [[Expedia Group]] to enrich the knowledge base.
*   **[[PMS marketplace integrations]]**: Already [[Cloudbeds]] and [[Mews]] certified, with [[Opera Cloud]] integration in progress.

### Weekly Product Planning
*   Weekly product planning sessions have commenced on Thursdays.
*   [[Fredrik]] retains ownership of the [[Product Roadmap]] and prioritization.
*   The feature list is currently tracked in [[Notion]].

### Client Feedback Highlights
*   Hotels prefer [[Daily reporting]] over weekly reports.
*   Demand for [[Custom theming]], including closed conversation bubbles and branded phrasing.
*   Room type specifications are often incomplete from scraping, highlighting a need for an [[IBE scraper]] for room products.
*   Hotels are inquiring about [[WhatsApp integration]] for in-stay communication.
*   [[Jory]] notes the critical importance of trust when engaging with hoteliers, who are characterized as particularly discerning buyers.
