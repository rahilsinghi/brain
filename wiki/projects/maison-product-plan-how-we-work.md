# Maison Product Plan: How We Work

This document outlines Maison's product planning cycle, from request capture to build, and details the current product tracks. It serves as a guide for how the team manages product development and prioritization.

## Key Concepts

Planning Cycle,Product Request Tracker,Grooming,Scoping,Product Tracks,Agentic Framework,Linear (Project Management),Notion (Tool)

## Details

The Maison product team follows a structured planning cycle to manage development:

### Planning Cycle
1.  **Capture**: The Commercial team logs all product requests in a dedicated [[Product Request Tracker]] within Notion. Each request gets its own row.
2.  **Groom**: Fredrik reviews the tracker and an overall Feature List. High-frequency or high-priority requests are then shaped into more defined features or larger epics.
3.  **Scope**: Features are formally specified, either through a Product Requirements Document (PRD) or lightweight requirements, and linked to a project or issue in [[Linear]]. The scope of a feature acts as a proxy for its size:
    *   **XL/L**: Indicates a multi-sprint epic.
    *   **M/S**: Denotes a standalone feature.
4.  **Build**: Work is prioritized and executed within ongoing cycles in [[Linear]], moving away from traditional sprint planning.

### Current Product Tracks
Each track focuses on a specific area of the Maison product:
*   **[[Agentic Framework]]** (Sandeep): Responsible for central orchestration, managing the tools registry, defining deployment profiles, and implementing deep actions within the Agentic UI (AG-UI).
*   **Public Chat on Maison.cx** (Kim): Covers the main website functionalities, investor demonstrations, and the customer support bot.
*   **Integrations** (Sandeep): Develops connections with external systems such as Property Management Systems (PMS), amenities providers, map services, and Yelp.
*   **Themes** (Kim): Manages the visual aspects of the product, including colors, layouts, breakpoints, border-radius, padding, margins, and fonts.
*   **Operator Reports** (Noel): Generates weekly emails and insights that can be used for social media posts.
*   **KB Quality & Scraping** (Noel/David): Focuses on improving the quality of the knowledge base and addressing missing QA pairs in the Business Console UI.
*   **Account Management** (David H): Handles features related to self-onboarding, contract management, and email notifications for accounts.
