# Karen Project: Escalation Ladder V2 Design

This document details the second version of the escalation ladder for the Karen Project, introducing a new 10-level structure with unique channels for each step. Key changes include the removal of deprecated communication methods and the integration of new capabilities like OSINT research, Slack, and real-time calendar and FedEx rate quote services.

## Key Concepts

Escalation Ladder,Multi-channel Communication,OSINT Research,FedEx Rate Quotes,Project Documentation

## Details

The Karen Project's escalation ladder has been redesigned to a Version 2, featuring a robust 10-level structure. A core principle of this new design is that each level utilizes a unique communication channel, preventing repetition and ensuring distinct escalation paths. The previous LinkedIn stub and the broken Twitter 402 integration have been removed from the ladder.

New capabilities integrated into the escalation sequence include:
- **Level 4 (L4):** OSINT (Open Source Intelligence) research, enhancing information gathering capabilities.
- **Level 6 (L6):** Slack integration, providing a dedicated internal communication channel for escalations.
- **Level 9 (L9):** Real Calendar integration, likely for scheduling and coordinating urgent actions.
- **Level 10 (L10):** FedEx rate quotes, suggesting a workflow involving urgent physical shipments or logistics coordination.

Accompanying these structural changes, the [[CLAUDE.md]] documentation for the project has been updated specifically in its ladder section. Environment variables and the accounts table have also been refreshed to reflect the new channels and their associated configurations. Furthermore, the de-escalation sequence within the project's operational procedures has been updated to align with the new 10-level ladder.
