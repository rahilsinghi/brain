# Qualcomm-EdgeMesh Collaboration for Windows App Store Submission

This article details the collaboration between Qualcomm and the NYU EdgeMesh AI team (Chinmay Shringi, Rahil Singhi, Manav Parikh, Bharath Gera, Sariya Khan) to bring the EdgeMesh app to the Windows App Store. Following the Snapdragon Multiverse Hackathon, Qualcomm provided loaner devices, technical support, and guidance, addressing challenges like cloud inference credits, model versioning, and MSIX packaging. The partnership aimed for app submission by March 31, 2026, with devices to be returned by April 17, 2026.

## Details

The email thread documents the ongoing collaboration between Qualcomm Technologies, Inc. and the NYU EdgeMesh AI team, comprising [[Chinmay Shringi]], [[Rahil Singhi]], Manav Parikh, [[Bharath Mahesh Gera]], and [[Sariya Khan]], with faculty advisor [[Alon Hillel-Tuch]]. This partnership aimed to transition the hackathon project, [[EdgeMesh – Distributed Edge Inference Mesh]], into a consumer-ready application for submission to the [[Windows App Store]].

**Key Aspects of the Collaboration:**

*   **Initial Opportunity (Feb 19, 2026):** [[Lauren Lunde]] from Qualcomm's [[Developer Relations]] team invited the EdgeMesh team to develop their app for the [[Windows App Store]], targeting a submission by March 31, 2026. The goal was to be among the first third-party apps for Snapdragon silicon on the store, with Qualcomm offering promotional support.
*   **Device Provisioning:** EdgeMesh initially expressed concerns about not having dedicated Qualcomm-powered Windows devices for continuous testing. Qualcomm responded by shipping loaner devices (laptop, phone, Uno Q) to the team, secured via a [[Loaner Agreement]]. The loaner Windows laptop required a [[BitLocker]] recovery key provided by Qualcomm to proceed with booting.
*   **Technical Support and Communication:** Qualcomm designated [[Kartikey Rawat]] and [[Shivay Lamba]] as primary technical contacts, with [[Derrick Johnson]] and [[Paul Torres]] also involved. A private [[Discord]] channel (`Columbia-hack-text-chat`) was set up for streamlined communication, debugging, and questions.
*   **Development Progress and Challenges (March 2026):**
    *   EdgeMesh successfully created a business account on the Windows Developer Partner Center using a provided coupon code.
    *   Significant app fixes were implemented, and local testing across macOS, Windows (on [[Snapdragon X Elite]]), and Android showed success.
    *   **Cloud Inference Credits:** A major blocker for testing the [[cloud inference]] pipeline was the lack of cloud credits. EdgeMesh requested approximately $100 in credits, seeking clarification on whether these would be [[Qualcomm AI Hub]] tokens or [[Foundry Local]] quota.
    *   **Model Versioning:** The team encountered a model versioning issue during the hackathon, where compiled models failed with [[QNN]] when using [[Genie]]. Qualcomm committed to following up with engineering on this matter.
    *   **Mobile Distribution:** EdgeMesh inquired about the feasibility of [[Flutter]] for iOS and Android mobile app distribution support.
    *   **MSIX Packaging:** Qualcomm recommended [[MSIX Packaging]] for Windows desktop app submission and provided an MSIX sample repository (`carrycooldude/onnx-msix-samples`) and guidance for app manifest and certification prep.
    *   **Coupon System for Users:** EdgeMesh proposed offering a small free tier (e.g., $50 per user) for their app's coupon system, seeking Qualcomm's feedback on reasonableness.
*   **Device Return:** By April 14, 2026, [[Lauren Lunde]] requested the return of the loaner devices by the end of that week (April 17) to prepare for another hackathon.
