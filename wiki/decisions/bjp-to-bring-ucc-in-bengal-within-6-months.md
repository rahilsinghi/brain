# BJP to Bring UCC in Bengal Within 6 Months

The Bharatiya Janata Party (BJP) has pledged to implement the Uniform Civil Code (UCC) in West Bengal within six months of forming government, as announced in their manifesto presented by Home Minister Amit Shah. This commitment highlights the party's focus on legal reforms and governance.

## Key Concepts

- **Uniform Civil Code (UCC):** A proposed legal framework aiming to standardize personal laws across religious communities in India.
- **BJP Manifesto:** The BJP's policy commitments and governance agenda, emphasizing UCC implementation in West Bengal.
- **West Bengal Governance:** The political and administrative implications of the BJP's promise to introduce UCC in the state.
- **Home Minister Amit Shah:** Key role in presenting the manifesto and advocating for the UCC.

## Details

The Bharatiya Janata Party (BJP) has announced its intention to implement the Uniform Civil Code (UCC) in West Bengal within six months of coming into power, as outlined in their manifesto. This pledge was presented by Home Minister Amit Shah, who emphasized the party's commitment to governance reforms, including the establishment of a secure border and zero tolerance for infiltration. The UCC aims to unify personal laws across different religious communities, replacing the existing system of religiously-based personal laws. This move is part of the BJP's broader strategy to promote a common legal framework, which has been a contentious issue in Indian politics. The manifesto also highlights other key priorities, such as ensuring national security and addressing cross-border challenges.
