# Refactor: Integration Intelligence Agent and Idea Power Sequence

This update to the Career-Datacenter repository refactors the project's 'ideas' section, merging former concepts into a unified 'Integration Intelligence Agent' with a three-phase lifecycle. It reorders ideas into an optimal 'power sequence' for conversation flow and reframes the Integration Intelligence Agent's focus from GTM tracking to data schema normalization, including specific advisories for discussions.

## Key Concepts

[[Integration Intelligence Agent]],[[Power Sequence]] (Idea Prioritization),[[Data Schema Normalization]],[[GTM Tracking]] (Go-To-Market),[[Recon Phase]],[[Drift Monitoring]],[[AI Discoverability]],[[DataChat]],[[NL Actions]]

## Details

This refactor in the [[Career-Datacenter]] repository (commit `c46b25b`) involved a significant restructuring of the project's core ideas section. The main changes include:

*   **Unification of Ideas**: Former ideas 1 and 2 were merged into a single, comprehensive concept: the **Integration Intelligence Agent**. This agent is defined by a three-phase lifecycle: Recon (reconnaissance), Schema Normalization, and Drift Monitoring.
*   **Optimal Conversation Sequence**: The ideas were reordered to follow a 'power sequence' designed for effective communication. The [[Integration Intelligence Agent]] was prioritized as the opening idea due to its grounded and operational nature. [[AI Discoverability]] was placed second to represent the ecosystem, followed by [[DataChat]]/[[NL Actions]] as product-focused concepts, with operational agents as trailing threads.
*   **Reframing the Integration Intelligence Agent**: The focus of the [[Integration Intelligence Agent]] was explicitly shifted from [[GTM Tracking]] (Go-To-Market tracking), which was identified as being outside the project's core domain and more aligned with `Conor Winders`'s expertise. Instead, its core function was reframed around data schema normalization, particularly relevant for integration data infrastructure at scale (e.g., 1,000+ marketplace adapters).
*   **Advisory Notes**: Explicit instructions were added, including an advisor's exact opening line for introducing the lead idea and a warning not to use the [[GTM Tracking]] framing when discussing with `Rogers`.
*   **Documentation Updates**: The conversation flow in 'Section 6' and the 'Quick Reference Card' were updated to reflect the new idea sequence and incorporate per-idea caveats.

This refactor was co-authored by [[Claude Sonnet 4.6]].
