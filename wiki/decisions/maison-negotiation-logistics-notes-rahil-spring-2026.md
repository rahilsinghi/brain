# Maison — Negotiation & Logistics Notes (Rahil, Spring 2026)

This article documents the key negotiation points and logistical considerations for Rahil's potential employment at Maison, an early-stage AI hospitality startup. The primary constraint is Rahil's F-1 visa immigration status, which prevents paid work until June 22, 2026, when OPT/EAD authorization begins. The notes outline a proposed solution involving a signing bonus for pre-authorization contributions and a formal offer letter before any work commences.

## Key Concepts

- **F-1 Visa / OPT (Optional Practical Training):** A post-graduation work authorization for international students in the US; Rahil's EAD has a June 22, 2026 start date
- **CPT (Curricular Practical Training):** A work authorization tied to active enrollment; both of Rahil's CPT authorizations are exhausted from prior Kismet internships
- **EAD (Employment Authorization Document):** The physical document authorizing OPT employment; referenced by employer in USCIS/I-20 paperwork
- **OPT 90-Day Unemployment Limit:** OPT holders may not accumulate more than 90 days of unemployment, making an early June 22 start preferable to July
- **Signing Bonus for Pre-Authorization Work:** A compensation mechanism to retroactively pay for contributions made before the official employment start date
- **Offer Letter as Mutual Protection:** A formal written agreement covering role, compensation, start date, and signing bonus clause — framed as protection for both parties, not distrust
- **Maison:** Early-stage AI platform for hoteliers, co-founded by Fredrik Sjoberg and Noel Panicker, fundraising in Q2

## Critical Issue: Work Authorization

Rahil is on an F-1 student visa and cannot legally receive pay for work in the US until his OPT EAD activates on **June 22, 2026**. Both CPT authorizations have been exhausted (used for Kismet internships: May–July 2025 and September–December 2025). Starting on an hourly basis in April is not legally permissible under the current immigration status.

---

## Proposed Solutions

### 1. Signing Bonus for Pre-EAD Contributions
Rahil is willing to participate in calls, design discussions, code reviews, and small projects before June 22. Any substantive work done in this period should be compensated **retroactively as a signing bonus** once employment officially begins on June 22. This arrangement must be:
- Documented in the offer letter **before any work begins**
- Framed positively: "I'd love to start contributing early, and here's how we can make that work legally."

> **Lesson from prior experience:** Rahil worked without a formal agreement at a prior employer (unnamed) expecting payment, and the offer was revoked at the last minute. A written agreement is non-negotiable before any work begins.

### 2. Adjusting the Start Date to June 22
Fredriks proposed full-time start of July should be moved up to **June 22**, which is Rahil's OPT/EAD activation date. Benefits:
- Earlier start is better for both parties
- The OPT clock begins regardless of whether Rahil is employed — delaying employment wastes the 90-day unemployment allowance
- Aligns with USCIS documentation requirements that reference a specific employer

---

## Offer Letter Requirements

A written offer letter must include:
1. **Role title and description**
2. **Compensation** — hourly rate for any pre-June-22 period; full-time salary and equity from June 22
3. **Official start date:** June 22, 2026
4. **Signing bonus clause** covering pre-June-22 contributions
5. **Standard employment terms**

**Framing for the ask:**
> "I want to make sure we're both protected and aligned on terms before I start contributing. A simple offer letter keeps things clean for both of us, especially since my immigration paperwork references a specific employer."
