The Bharatiya Janata Party (BJP) has pledged to implement the Uniform Civil Code (UCC) in West Bengal within six months of forming government, as part of its manifesto presented by Home Minister Amit Shah. The announcement highlights the party's commitment to legal uniformity and border security.

## Details

In a significant political move, the Bharatiya Janata Party (BJP) has announced its intention to bring the Uniform Civil Code (UCC) into effect in West Bengal within six months if the party forms the government. This pledge was made public as part of the BJP's manifesto, presented by Home Minister Amit Shah. The UCC aims to establish a common set of laws governing personal matters such as marriage, divorce, inheritance, and adoption, applicable to all citizens regardless of religion. The announcement underscores the BJP's focus on legal uniformity and governance efficiency, aligning with its broader national agenda. The manifesto also emphasized other key promises, including ensuring secure borders and adopting a zero-tolerance policy towards infiltration. This move is expected to have significant implications for the legal and social framework of West Bengal, potentially reshaping civil laws and administrative practices in the state.
