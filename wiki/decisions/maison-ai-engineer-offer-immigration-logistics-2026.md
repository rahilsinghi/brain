**Proposed Contract Structure & Immigration Concerns (April 13, 2026)**

[[Fredrik Sjoberg]] proposes a two-document approach to avoid immigration paperwork complications:
1.  An **offer letter** for a full-time AI Engineer role starting June 22, including compensation and sign-on bonus, intended for [[USCIS]] use.
2.  A **trial period agreement** for April-June, covering expectations during the ramp-up phase.

The proposed compensation for the trial period is $50/hour, up to 20 hours/week, paid as a sign-on bonus upon full-time start. [[Rahil Singhi]] confirms the proposed dates:
*   Trial period: April 16 - May 15
*   Graduation: May 18, 2026
*   Full-time start (OPT/EAD): June 22, 2026

[[Fredrik Sjoberg]] then confirms the compensation would be $7k/month, with the sign-on bonus terms moved to the trial period contract to avoid issues with [[USCIS]] submissions. He also clarifies that the engagement will initially be structured as an **independent contractor (1099)** arrangement, as [[Maison]] does not currently have W-2 employees. He asks [[Rahil Singhi]] to flag any issues this might cause for OPT/EAD.

**Rahil Singhi's Response: Immigration Path & Availability (April 13, 2026)**

[[Rahil Singhi]] confirms acceptance of $7k/month and the sign-on bonus handling. [[Rahil Singhi]] explains that while **1099 works for the initial OPT period**, a **W-2 employer-employee relationship with an E-Verify enrolled employer is required for the STEM OPT extension (24 months) and H-1B sponsorship**. This means a conversion to W-2 would be necessary before the initial 12-month OPT window closes to maintain a clear immigration path. [[Rahil Singhi]] notes that there's no immediate urgency but advises keeping it on the radar as [[Maison]] scales. [[Rahil Singhi]] offers to discuss the timeline further.

Regarding availability, [[Rahil Singhi]] states that there will be travel between graduation (May 18) and June 9, but work will continue with regular updates. From June 9 to 19, [[Rahil Singhi]] will be mostly off the grid due to a cruise, returning to full-time work on June 22.
