# Maison — Negotiation & Logistics Notes (Rahil / Fredrik)

Rahil is negotiating a full-time role at Maison, an early-stage AI hospitality startup, following outreach from CEO Fredrik Sjoberg. Key issues include a work authorization gap due to F-1/OPT status that prevents paid work before June 22, 2026, and the need for a formal written offer letter before any pre-start contributions begin. The goal is to align on a June 22 official start date, a signing bonus for pre-EAD contributions, and clear written terms to protect both parties.

## Key Concepts

- **F-1 Visa / OPT (Optional Practical Training):** Post-graduation US work authorization for international students; Rahil's EAD has an approved start date of June 22, 2026
- **CPT (Curricular Practical Training):** Work authorization tied to academic enrollment; both CPT windows are exhausted (Kismet summer and fall 2025 internships)
- **EAD (Employment Authorization Document):** The physical card granting legal work authorization; cannot work for pay in the US before this date
- **90-Day Unemployment Limit:** OPT has a cap on unemployment days; starting June 22 avoids wasting any of the window
- **Signing Bonus for Pre-EAD Work:** Mechanism to retroactively compensate pre-June 22 contributions once employment officially begins
- **Offer Letter as Mutual Protection:** Written terms protect both Rahil and the employer, and are required for USCIS/I-20 documentation
- **Maison:** Early-stage AI platform for hoteliers, founded by Fredrik Sjoberg (CEO) and Noel Panicker (Co-Founder)

## Details

## Situation Overview

On March 27, Fredrik Sjoberg (CEO, Maison) emailed asking whether Rahil could begin on an hourly basis in April, with a transition to full-time by July. This timeline conflicts with Rahil's current immigration status and requires direct, constructive negotiation.

---

## Critical Issues

### 1. Work Authorization Gap — No Paid Work Before June 22

Rahil is on an F-1 student visa, graduating NYU MS Computer Engineering in May 2026. Both CPT authorizations have been exhausted:
- **Summer 2025:** Kismet internship (May–July 2025)
- **Fall 2025:** Kismet internship (September–December 2025)

OPT/EAD has been applied for with an **official start date of June 22, 2026**. Until that date, Rahil legally cannot receive compensation for work performed in the United States. Starting hourly in April is not legally viable.

---

### 2. Proposed Solution — Signing Bonus for Pre-EAD Contributions

Rahil is willing and enthusiastic to engage before June 22 (calls, design discussions, code reviews, lightweight projects). However, any substantive pre-start contribution must be:
- Acknowledged in a **written offer letter before work begins**
- Compensated retroactively as a **signing bonus**, paid once employment officially commences on June 22

**Critical precedent:** At a prior employer (unnamed), Rahil performed work without a formal agreement, the offer was revoked at the last minute, and that work went uncompensated. A written agreement before contributions begin is non-negotiable.

---

### 3. Start Date — June 22, Not July

Fredrik proposed full-time by July, but June 22 is better for both parties:
- It is the first legal day Rahil can be paid
- The OPT 90-day unemployment clock starts ticking regardless of actual employment; an earlier start protects Rahil's status
- Maison gets an earlier contributor

**Ask:** Formally set June 22, 2026 as the full-time start date.

---

### 4. Offer Letter Requirements

The written offer letter should include:
- Role title and description
- Compensation structure (hourly rate for any pre-FT period; full-time salary and/or equity from June 22)
- Official start date: **June 22, 2026**
- Signing bonus clause covering pre-June 22 contributions
- Standard employment terms

Framing for the ask: *"I want to make sure we're both protected and aligned on terms before I start contributing. A simple offer letter keeps things clean for both of us, especially since my immigration paperwork references a specific employer."*

Note: OPT requires employer information on the I-20/EAD — a formal offer supports USCIS documentation.

---

- Preference: A call to discuss rather than email
- **Available:** Sunday March 29 (after 1 PM ET) or Monday March 30 (flexible)
- **Unavailable:** Sunday March 29 at 11:30 AM ET (Brenton/Toolhouse call)

## Tone & Communication Guidelines

- Express genuine enthusiasm for Maison's mission and the opportunity

- Do **not** mention Kismet by name; reference the lesson learned: *"I've learned the importance of having terms in writing before starting work"
