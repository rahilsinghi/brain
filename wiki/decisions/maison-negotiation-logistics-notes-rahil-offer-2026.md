# Maison — Negotiation & Logistics Notes (Rahil Offer, 2026)

This document outlines the key negotiation points and logistical considerations for Rahil's potential employment at Maison, an early-stage AI hospitality startup. Central issues include F-1 visa work authorization constraints that prevent paid work before June 22, 2026, a proposed signing bonus to compensate pre-EAD contributions, and the need for a formal written offer letter before any work begins. The goal is to align on a June 22 full-time start date and protect both parties legally and professionally.

## Key Concepts

- **F-1 Visa / OPT (Optional Practical Training):** Rahil's current immigration status; EAD start date is June 22, 2026, which is the earliest legal date for paid employment
- **CPT (Curricular Practical Training):** Both CPT authorizations are fully exhausted from prior Kismet internships (summer and fall 2025)
- **EAD (Employment Authorization Document):** Filed with June 22 start date; required before any US paid work can begin
- **Signing Bonus Structure:** Mechanism to retroactively compensate pre-EAD contributions once employment officially begins on June 22
- **OPT 90-Day Unemployment Limit:** OPT clock begins regardless of employment start; starting June 22 avoids wasting authorized work time
- **Written Offer Letter:** Required before any work begins; must include role, compensation, start date, signing bonus clause, and standard employment terms
- **Maison:** Early-stage AI platform for hoteliers, co-founded by Fredrik Sjoberg and Noel Panicker, fundraising in Q2 2026

## Details

## Situation Overview

On March 27, Fredrik Sjoberg (CEO of Maison) emailed asking whether Rahil could begin on an hourly basis in April, transitioning to full-time by July. This timeline conflicts with Rahil's current immigration status and requires careful, direct communication.

## Critical Issue 1: Work Authorization

Rahil is an NYU MS Computer Engineering student on an F-1 visa, graduating May 2026. Both CPT authorizations have been exhausted:
- **Summer 2025:** Kismet internship (May–July)
- **Fall 2025:** Kismet internship (September–December)

An OPT/EAD application has been filed with a **June 22, 2026 start date**. Until that date, Rahil **cannot legally receive compensation for work performed in the United States**. Starting hourly in April is not legally permissible under the current immigration status.

## Critical Issue 2: Signing Bonus for Pre-EAD Contributions

Rahil is willing and enthusiastic about contributing before June 22 through calls, design discussions, code reviews, and small projects. However, any substantive work performed before the EAD start date must be:
- Acknowledged in writing **before any work begins**
- Compensated retroactively via a **signing bonus** once employment officially starts on June 22

**This must be formalized in a written offer letter in advance.** A prior experience (not to be named explicitly) involved working without a formal agreement and having an offer revoked at the last minute. That situation must not be repeated.

> Framing: *"I've learned the importance of having terms in writing before starting work."

## Critical Issue 3: Start Date — June 22, Not July

Fredrk indicated a July full-time start date, but June 22 is both possible and preferable:
