# Rahil Singhi's Employment Offer and Immigration Planning at Maison

This article details the employment offer extended by Fredrik Sjoberg of Maison to Rahil Singhi, covering compensation, contract structure, and crucial immigration considerations. The discussions involve differentiating between independent contractor (1099) and W-2 employment for OPT, STEM OPT, and H-1B visa processes, along with establishing clear start dates and a trial period.

## Details

The email thread documents the negotiation and structuring of an employment offer from [[Maison]] CEO [[Fredrik Sjoberg]] to [[Rahil Singhi]] for an AI Engineer role. Key points of discussion and agreement include:

**Offer Terms & Compensation:**
*   **Role:** AI Engineer
*   **Monthly Compensation:** $7,000/month for full-time employment.
*   **Trial Period Compensation:** $50/hour, up to 20 hours/week, paid as part of the sign-on bonus upon full-time start.
*   **Sign-on Bonus:** To be included in the trial period contract rather than the USCIS-related offer letter for cleaner immigration paperwork.

**Contract Structure and Immigration:**
*   **Initial Structure:** The engagement is initially proposed as an independent contractor (1099) arrangement, as [[Maison]] does not currently have employees on payroll.
*   **Immigration Concerns:** [[Rahil Singhi]] highlighted critical requirements for international students on Optional Practical Training (OPT):
    *   The initial 12-month OPT period can accommodate 1099, but it must be verifiable employment.
    *   The STEM OPT extension (24 months) and subsequent H-1B visa sponsorship *require* a W-2 employer-employee relationship with an E-Verify enrolled employer.
    *   Conversion to W-2 would be necessary before the initial 12-month OPT window closes to maintain a clean immigration path.
*   **Fredrik's Response:** Acknowledged the need to convert to W-2 employment for future immigration stages and confirmed willingness to address it as [[Maison]] scales.
*   **Document Strategy:** To simplify USCIS paperwork, [[Fredrik Sjoberg]] proposed two separate documents:
    *   An **offer letter** for the full-time AI Engineer role starting June 22, including compensation and the sign-on bonus, intended for USCIS.
    *   A **trial period agreement** covering expectations and logistics for the April–June period.

**Timeline and Availability:**
*   **Trial Period:** April 16, 2026, through May 15, 2026.
*   **Graduation:** May 18, 2026.
*   **Full-time Start (OPT/EAD):** June 22, 2026.
*   **Rahil's Travel:** [[Rahil Singhi]] will be traveling between graduation and June 9, and then mostly off the grid on a cruise from June 9 to June 19, returning full-time on June 22, 2026.

**Motivation for Written Agreement:**
*   [[Rahil Singhi]] requested a written offer letter outlining terms, hours, rate, and sign-on bonus, citing a past negative experience where a verbal agreement was revoked, leading to unpaid work. This written agreement is also crucial for OPT paperwork with USCIS.
