## Details

The email thread, spanning from April 8 to April 14, 2026, outlines the formalization of Rahil Singhi's employment as an AI Engineer with Maison.

Rahil Singhi expresses excitement after a technical discussion with Sandeep, highlighting interest in "knowledge graph work and the Claude-optimized development approach." **Proposed Employment Structure (April 13)**
Fredrik Sjoberg proposes a two-document approach to streamline the process, especially for Rahil's OPT paperwork:
1.  **Offer Letter**: For the full-time AI Engineer role starting June 22, including compensation and the sign-on bonus, intended for USCIS.
2.  **Trial Period Agreement**: Covering expectations for the April-June ramp-up period.

The trial period was specified as April 16 through May 15, with a rate of $50/hour, up to 20 hours/week, to be paid as a sign-on bonus upon full-time commencement. Rahil's graduation was May 18, 2026.

**Compensation and Employment Type (April 13)**
Fredrik confirms a compensation of $7,000/month for the full-time role, with the sign-on bonus terms moved to the trial period contract to avoid USCIS complications. He clarifies that the engagement will initially be as an independent contractor (1099), as Maison does not currently have W-2 employees.

**Immigration Implications and Availability (April 13)**
Rahil confirms the compensation and trial terms. He outlines the immigration requirements: while 1099 is acceptable for the initial OPT period, the STEM OPT extension (24 months) and H-1B sponsorship mandate a W-2 employer-employee relationship with an E-Verify enrolled employer. This necessitates a conversion to W-2 before the initial 12-month OPT window closes. Rahil also informs Fredrik of his travel plans, including a cruise from June 9-19 (mostly off-grid), with a full-time return on June 22.

**Onboarding & Next Steps (April 14)**
Following the signed agreement, Fredrik instructs Rahil to set up his Maison email and provides an invitation to Linear, Maison's task management system.
